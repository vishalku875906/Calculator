How EMI Calculator Works

EMI Formula

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1)

Where:

  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100)
  • n = Number of monthly instalments

Key Concepts

Principal Amount

The original loan amount you borrowed from the lender.

Interest Rate

The annual percentage rate (APR) charged by the lender for the loan.

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment)

Fixed monthly payment including both principal and interest components.

Total Interest

Total interest paid over the loan period. Calculated as (EMI × n) - Principal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using a standard financial formula that considers the principal amount, interest rate, and loan tenure. The formula ensures equal monthly payments throughout the loan period.

Can I reduce my EMI?

Yes, you can reduce EMI by either increasing the loan tenure or by paying a larger down payment to reduce the principal amount.

What is prepayment?

Prepayment refers to paying part or all of your remaining loan amount before the scheduled maturity date. This helps reduce the total interest payable.
