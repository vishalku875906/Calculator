What is BMI and why it matters

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple index of weight-for-height commonly used to classify underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obesity. It is widely used in population studies and clinical screening because it provides a quick estimate of body fat for most adults.

How BMI is calculated

BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))². While BMI is a useful screening tool, it does not measure body fat directly and may misclassify very muscular individuals or people with atypical body composition.

Interpreting results

Use the category readout to determine whether you fall into underweight, normal, overweight, or obese ranges. For clinical advice or personalized recommendations consult a healthcare professional; this tool provides educational guidance only.

Tips for healthy weight

  • Combine regular physical activity with balanced nutrition.
  • Track progress — small, consistent changes lead to long-term results.
  • Consult professionals for personalized plans, especially if you have underlying health conditions.

FAQ

Is BMI accurate for children and teens?

No — children and adolescents require age- and sex-specific BMI percentiles rather than the adult BMI categories used here.

Can athletes have high BMI but low body fat?

Yes — muscular athletes may have a higher BMI while having low body fat. Use body composition measures for more accuracy.
